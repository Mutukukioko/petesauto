Understanding Your Car

Engine Light On

Wondering why your car check engine light is on? Worry no more, we got you covered. Check engine light disturbs many drivers, as it means something could be wrong with your engine and needs to be checked as soon as possible.

Check engine light will come on due to the following reasons:

Oxygen sensors (O2 sensors) measure the quantity of unburnt oxygen in a car's exhaust system. It is linked with the vehicle's computer system where it sends information used to control the mixture of air and fuel that gets into the cylinders.

A failed O2 sensor can cause increased fuel consumption and potential damage to other components like spark plugs. If you notice a problem with your O2 sensor, it should be replaced as soon as possible.

Your gas cap prevents petroleum vapor from escaping into the air. If your gas tank is left open, the Check Engine light will automatically come on after a short while.

The Catalytic Converter prevents the emission of hydrocarbons and other harmful substances by converting them into Carbon (IV) oxide and Water vapor. Your O2 sensors monitor the performance of the Catalytic Converter, and if it becomes inefficient, it will trigger the Check Engine light.

Spark plugs are responsible for igniting your engine. Worn out spark plugs or wires can trigger the Check Engine warning.

The Mass Airflow Sensor (MAF) measures the amount of air entering your engine, which determines the required fuel for optimal performance. A faulty Mass Airflow Sensor can negatively affect engine performance.


How to Solve Check Engine Problems


An OBD scanner can be helpful in diagnosing and solving Check Engine warning problems. An OBD Scanner (Onboard Diagnostic) is a car diagnostic gadget that reads the error memory and data from your vehicle's system. It can help you identify and fix problems or clear the errors.
