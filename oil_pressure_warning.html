Understanding Your Car

Oil Pressure Warning.

Your dashboard is like the 'notice board' for all information that your car needs to pass to you. Old age cars used to communicate through lights and icons displayed on the dashboard. Modern cars simply display the information in words. This mostly happens with the new hybrid generation cars.

Noticed your Oil Pressure light is on?

What does it mean?

When the Oil Pressure warning lights, it is advisable to switch off your engine. This is to prevent your engine from being badly damaged if it runs on low or no oil at all. Always ensuring that your engine has enough oil is one of the simple ways to maintain and extend your engine's lifespan and performance. Your dashboard might show an Oil Pressure Warning if the oil level is extremely low or more than the required potential.

Reasons why the Oil Pressure Warning shows on your dashboard;

One of the tips for car maintenance is to always check your car before igniting it. Make it a daily routine to check your oil level before switching it on.

How to check your oil level.
  1. Open your bonnet.
  2. Remove your oil dip stick.
  3. The dip stick is labeled L and F (Low & Full).
  4. Wipe it with a clean cloth and insert it back.
  5. Remove it again and check the oil stains between L and F marks.

To be safe, the oil stain should be at F or slightly below it.

For your engine to run smoothly, you should ensure you service it regularly and also flush it when need be. You should change your oil and other worn-out spare parts each time you go for a service. Failing to change your oil, could result into low oil pressure.
Dirty oil may also cause lots of deposits and debris in your engine, thus your the Oil Pressure warning will illuminate. Engine flush cleans away the oil deposits and debris before pouring in the clean oil.

Oil pump regulates the oil pressure. With a faulty oil pump, your engine oil pressure will decrease. The decreased oil pressure will turn on the Oil Pressure Warning light.

If you already serviced your engine and pump but the Oil Pressure warning is still on, then the sensor could be worn out and should be replaced.



Conclusion

In conclusion, dashboard warning lights are critical indicators that should never be ignored. It is essential to familiarize yourself with the various warning lights your car may have and what they mean, as they can help prevent more significant problems from occurring. Ignoring these warning lights could lead to costly repairs or even more severe issues that can jeopardize your safety. Regular maintenance of your car can also help prevent warning lights from flashing on your dashboard in the first place. As always, take good care of your vehicle, and it will take care of you.
