Understanding Your Car

In addition to the common dashboard warning lights that indicate important vehicle functions such as the 'Check Engine Light', oil pressure, and anti-lock braking system (ABS), modern vehicles often have a variety of other dashboard lights that alert and warn drivers in case of a problem with the vehicle's system. These additional dashboard lights are crucial in providing drivers with important information about their vehicle's condition, ensuring their safety and also preventing potential damages to the vehicle.

Here are some of the other warning lights that may be displayed on your dashboard;


  1. Clogged Air Filter Warning.

    A clogged air filter icon on your dashboard simply indicates that your engine air filter is extremely dirty and needs replacement. The air filter is responsible for sieving dirt,dust and other particles from the air entering the engine. It also ensures that your engine runs smoothly. Your air filter can get clogged overtime with dirt and debris, reducing the flow of air Into the engine and also increasing fuel consumption. This can lead to poor engine performance and also trigger the Check Engine light.

    To sort out this issue, you should replace your air filter as soon as possible.

  2. Airbag Warning.

    The airbag warning icon simply looks like a person on a car seat, with a circle in front of them. It may also include the word "AIRBAG" or "SRS"(Supplemental Restraint System)

    This warning light may appear on your dashboard to inform you that there could be a problem with your car's airbag system. This could be a malfunction or a faulty sensor. The airbag is a crucial car safety component; therefore, it is important to get your car airbag system checked and problem sorted as soon as possible.

  3. Steering Wheel Warning.

    The Steering Wheel warning icon on your dashboard is simply an indication of a problem with your car's power steering system. This could be due to various reasons such as; a malfunctioning power steering pump, an empty power steering reservoir, or a problem with your steering belt. It is dangerous to drive with a faulty power steering system, therefore, you should get it checked before proceeding further.

  4. Fuel Warning.

    The fuel warning icon is an important feature in modern vehicles. It is basically meant to alert drivers when they are running short of fuel. This icon simply appears on the dashboard and is accompanied by an audible warning.

    Running out of fuel can cause stalling or even damaging the engine. To avoid these problems, you should refuel as soon as possible.


Remember

Overally, it's important to address any dashboard warning lights promptly and have your system inspected by a professional to ensure your safety on the road.
