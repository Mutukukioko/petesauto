Car Care Products

  • interior-care
  • Exterior Car Detailing.

Car care products are items and substances that are used for maintaining and protecting a vehicle's appearance and working condition. These products include cleaning products, wax, polish, tyre shine, leather and upholstery cleaners, engine degreasers, and so much more. Car care products are essential for keeping your car's exterior clean and protected from harsh weather conditions, environmental pollutants, and wear and tear caused by regular use.

Proper maintenance and use of car care products not only adds value to your vehicle but also boosts its appearance and extends its lifespan.

This article sheds light on car care products from basic car washing and waxing to more advanced products like polishes, coatings, and sealants. There is a wide range of car care products available that cater to different needs and budgets. From cleaning to protecting and restoring, these products help you keep your car in top condition, both aesthetically and mechanically.

Car care products can be classified into two; Exterior and Interior car care products.

Exterior Car Detailing.

Exterior car cleaning is an essential part of vehicle maintenance that not only enhances the overall appearance of the car but also protects it from damage caused by dirt, dust, and grime. Keeping the exterior of your car clean helps preserve the paint, prevent rust, and maintain its value over time. From hand-washing to automatic car washes, there are several effective ways to clean your car's exterior, and with the advancement of cleaning products and techniques, keeping your car looking new has never been easier.

Car Wash

When washing your car, it's advisable to use car soap, but situations may arise where you don't have access to it. This may happen after exposure to mud or bird droppings. It's essential to know what substitutes you can use instead of car soap and what to avoid. In this article, we'll explore the best car soap alternatives and ones that are not recommended.


Which soap or detergent can I use to wash my car?

Hand Soap.

Despite what most people believe, hand soap can serve as a viable substitute for car washing soap. However, it's essential to be cautious and only use a mild solution of hand soap instead of a harsh one. If you opt for strong hand soap, it may leave an undesirable film or residue on your vehicle.

Shampoo.

In situations where you are on a road trip and unable to find a car wash, there is a useful alternative to clean your car. You can use a bottle of hair shampoo that you have packed in your travel bag. While it may not be the best for removing stubborn dirt and grime, it can still be effective in cleaning your car. Just be sure to use it in moderation and follow up by applying a new layer of wax after washing.

Toilet Cleaner.

It is common to encounter stubborn stains on your car that just won't go away, and some may even seem permanent. While toilet cleaners may effectively remove these difficult stains from your car's body, it is important to exercise caution when using them frequently. This is because these cleaners can potentially harm the protective wax and coating on your car, eventually leading to fading of the car paint over time. Therefore, it is advisable to use alternative cleaning methods that are safe for your car's exterior.


It is important to ensure that the soap being used to wash your car is specifically designed for cars and their paint, as this will prevent any potential damage from harsh chemicals. While hand soap, shampoo or toilet cleaner may be a temporary solution, they should not be used regularly as they may not be suitable for your car's finish.


What kind of soap should I not wash my car with?
  1. Dish soap.

    Even though dish soaps are tough on grease, they are not suitable for washing your car since their chemical composition can damage the protective wax and clear coat. Using mild dish soap can even worsen the situation. Therefore, it is best to avoid using dish soap for car washing purposes and opt for alternative methods that are safer for your vehicle. Let your dish soap be reserved for washing dishes only.


  2. Laundry detergent.

    Although laundry detergent is less harsh than dish soap, it can still damage the protective coating of your car. The chemicals and degreasers present in laundry detergent are designed to remove stubborn stains from clothes, not to clean cars. If you use laundry detergent excessively on your vehicle, it will gradually lose its shine and become dull.



Car Exterior Coatings.

Car exterior coatings are specialized formulas that are designed to enhance the appearance of vehicles. They protect the car paint surface from scratches, stains, dirt, road debris, and other damages. They can be applied either as a liquid coating or as a polymer film over the paint surface. Depending on the specific type of coating, they can last from a few weeks to several years, making them a popular choice for not only car enthusiasts, but also for car owners who want to maintain the value of their vehicles.

They come in various forms like wax, sealants, ceramic coatings, and paint protection film, which are discussed below:

  1. )  Paint Protection Film(PPF)

    Paint protection film is a thin transparent film that is applied to the exterior of a vehicle's paint to protect it from scratches, chips, and other types of damage. It is typically made from a durable and flexible material such as polyurethane or polymer that is designed to be resistant to abrasion and impact. The film is applied by a professional installer who carefully aligns it to the contours of the vehicle's body to provide maximum coverage. Paint protection film is an excellent investment for anyone who wants to preserve the appearance of their vehicle and protect it from everyday wear and tear.

    Paint protection helps prevent your car's paint from fading, which can save you money in the long run. Dirt, dust and other particles can stick to your car's paint, and trying to clean it can result in damage to the paint job. With paint protection film, you can easily wipe away debris using super absorbent towels, instead of having to wash and scrub your car to restore its shine.



  2. )  Ceramic Coating.

    Ceramic coating is a highly durable protective layer made from a blend of ceramic particles and resins, which is applied in a liquid form and then cured to form a hard and glossy finish. It is commonly used on vehicles to safeguard against scratches, environmental elements, and UV damage. As the coating creates a chemical bond with the car's paint, it provides a hydrophobic layer that ensures water and dirt slide off the surface with ease. In addition to long-lasting protection, ceramic coatings help to maintain the newness and shine of the vehicle's paint. It is important to note that regular cleaning and maintenance are required to keep the ceramic coating in optimal condition and ensure the protection of the car's paint.



  3. )  Waxes and Sealants.

    Waxes and sealants are two different types of products used to protect the finish of a vehicle. Waxes are typically made of natural or synthetic ingredients and come in a paste or liquid form. They are designed to add a layer of protection over the paint work, helping to repel water, dirt and other contaminants. Waxes are applied either by hand or with a machine, and can also provide a deep gloss to the paint work.



    Sealants, on the other hand, are typically made of synthetic polymers and provide a longer lasting protection compared to wax. They are formulated to form an invisible layer of protection that bonds with the paint work, creating a barrier against contaminants and UV rays. Sealants can also provide a high level of shine, similar to that of a wax.Both waxes and sealants have their pros and cons, and many car enthusiasts prefer to use a combination of both for the best protection and shine. It ultimately comes down to personal preference and the type of maintenance and protection you want for your vehicle.



Tyre and Wheel Care.


Tyre Cleaning.

Maintaining a clean car is crucial to avoid rust formation that can compromise the safety of the vehicle. While you do need to clean the tyres of your car, it's important to note that you cannot simply throw them into a washing machine for a quick and rough cleaning cycle. Instead, you need a specialized cleaning solution and a meticulous detailing process to ensure that your tyres, rims, and hubs are thoroughly clean and sparkling.



How to keep your tyres clean and superb.

Before cleaning and scrubbing, it is advisable to inspect every tyre, particularly when washing 4x4 or light truck tyres that carry heavier loads. Take note of any foreign objects such as stones, rocks, leaves, or other debris that may have become lodged in the tyre tread. This is an ideal opportunity to eliminate these items and preserve the quality of your tyres.

The following steps will guide you on how to clean and dress your vehicle's tyres:

  1. Gather your tyre cleaning tools and products.

    Gather all the necessary items to clean your tyres, such as a sponge, bucket, tyre brush, and your preferred tyre cleaning product. An alternative solution can be made using a capful of dishwashing soap in a bucket of water, which effectively removes stubborn dirt from your tyres.

  2. Clean your tyres.

    To clean your tyres thoroughly, begin by washing them one at a time. If you're using a bucket, change the water after each tyre to avoid soaking your tyre in dirty water. Next, rinse off any visible dirt using a water hose or pressure washer. Be sure to focus on the wheel rim and wheel wells, removing any brake dust accumulation as well. Once all the dirt and debris is removed, apply the recommended tyre cleaner and let it sit for the designated amount of time to loosen any remaining grime.

  3. Scrub your tyres and rims using a brush.

    To clean your tyres effectively, use a bristled brush to scrub away all the dirt and debris. For the rims, it's recommended to use a softer wheel brush to prevent any scratches. If you used a water-based solution, you may need to put in extra effort. Make sure to check the entire tyre surface to ensure you haven't missed any spots. Afterwards, rinse everything thoroughly to avoid leaving any cleaning solution behind.


  4. Dry your tyres.

    To prevent any marks or lines on your tyres and wheels, it's essential to use a fresh microfiber cloth to dry your tyres and rims. Ensure that the cloth is free of any debris or dirt, and your tyres must be entirely spotless, leaving a glossy finish.

  5. Apply tyre shine to your tyres.

    To safeguard your tyres and give them a sleek appearance with a lustrous shine, it is recommended to apply wax, tyre black or tyre dressing after ensuring that they are fully dry. These products will act as a protective shield against potential harm while enhancing the aesthetics of your tyres.

In conclusion, keeping your tyres clean is not only vital to maintaining a vehicle's appearance, but also plays a significant role in ensuring its safety. Regular cleaning prevents the accumulation of harmful debris that can cause damage to the tyres and affect their performance on the road. Simple techniques such as using soap and water or investing in specialised tyre cleaning products can go a long way in preserving your tyres and prolonging their lifespan. Therefore, make a habit of cleaning your tyres regularly, and you're sure to enjoy a safer, smoother, and more enjoyable driving experience every time.



Tyre shine products.


Tyre shine products are a type of automotive detailing product that is used to enhance the appearance of a vehicle's tyres. These products typically come in the form of a spray or gel and are applied to the tyres after they have been cleaned. The main purpose of tyre shine products is to give the tyres a deep, glossy shine that makes them new and well-maintained. Some tyre shine products also contain materials that help to protect the tyres from UV damage and other forms of wear and tear. However, it is important to note that these products should only be used on the sidewalls of the tyres and should never be applied to the tread, as it can reduce traction and could create a safety hazard.

Why cleaning your vehicle's tyres is beneficial.

Car manufacturers and sellers highly recommend that you regularly clean your tyres, ideally once a month or once every two to three months. By doing so, you will not only prolong the life of your tyres, but also ensure that they perform optimally. Essentially, it is a task that is worthwhile and should not be overlooked.

The following are some of the benefits of cleaning your car tyres:

  1. )  Safety and better performance.

    Ensuring that your vehicle's tyres are kept clean is pivotal to maintaining road safety. This is due to the fact that clean tyres provide superior traction and braking ability, thus minimizing the risk of accidents and loss of control on the road. Additionally, dirt and dust left on tyres overtime can erode the protective coating, promoting metal corrosion and premature damage.

    By keeping your tyres clean, you can also safeguard them from cracking, weathering and corrosion that can be caused by hazardous contaminants. Neglecting to clean your tyres regularly can lead to their deterioration at an accelerated pace. Hence, it is advisable to regularly clean your tyres to prevent an accumulation of damaging particles.

  2. )  Pleasing appearance.

    Having sparkling clean tyres is more important than you might realize as it significantly enhances the appearance and overall feel of your vehicle. Scrubbing away dirt and grime from your tyres creates an improved visual appeal, making your car look as if it just rolled off the production line.

    Regardless of whether you drive on a daily basis or not, the sight of dirt or mud on your wheels is unappealing to many people.

  3. )  Easy cleaning.

    Frequently cleaning the tyres of your car ensures that it stays in top-notch state without the need for extensive cleaning in the future. This routine maintenance will improve the performance of your car and extend its lifespan more than cars that don't receive regular cleaning.

  4. )  Prevents breakdown.

    Cleaning your car tyres is crucial to avoid breakdowns as the accumulation of dirt and debris can cause damage and rusting of the parts, which eventually weakens them and compromises your control over the vehicle. Therefore, by regularly cleaning your tyres, you can prevent such mishaps from occurring and ensure your car's durability in challenging road and weather conditions.

  5. )  Efficiency in driving.

    Having clean tyres can make it easier for you to drive your car on bumpy roads, whereas driving a car that has not been cleaned in a while could lead to various issues.



Glass Polishing.

To remove scratches and stains from glass, it is recommended to use a glass polishing compound along with a glass polishing towel or pad. This combination effectively treats the affected areas and restores glass to its original shining condition. However, if you prefer to polish the glass manually, this method primarily aims at cleaning rather than scratch removal.

Glass polishing is a crucial step before applying a ceramic glass sealant or coating. A glass cleaner may make the glass clear, but it could still be bonded with contaminants on a microscopic level. These contaminants can hinder the bonding process between the glass and the sealant or coating. Therefore, it is essential to properly prepare the glass by using a glass polish to ensure the longevity of the applied sealant or coating. Over time, exterior glass surfaces accumulate various substances such as water spots, mineral deposits, calcium build-up, and residue from wiper blades. To restore the glass to its original condition, it is necessary to polish it and remove these unwanted elements.



How to protect your car from sun-scorching.

The sun's ultraviolet rays can cause damage to your vehicle, from paint peeling to battery damage. It is important to take care of your car just as you take care of yourself in the heat. To protect your investment, we have compiled tips for safeguarding your vehicle from the sun.

  1. )  Regular washing and waxing.

    Just as you protect your skin from the harmful effects of the sun with sunscreen, you should also take steps to protect your car from the sun's damaging rays. Applying wax to your car's exterior can help to preserve its paint job by creating a barrier against the sun and locking in natural oils. Additionally, it's important to clean your car regularly to remove dried bugs and other debris that can cause fading and chipping in the heat. To avoid damage, it's recommended that you use an ultra-absorbent towel to dry your car after washing it.


  2. )  Parking under the shade or in the garage.

    While it may seem obvious, many of us tend to overlook the benefit of parking in the shade. It's crucial to keep in mind that parking in a shaded area or garage can save us a lot of trouble. Spending a few extra minutes to locate a well-shaded spot for your vehicle can pay off in the long run. Additionally, buying a sunshade can be a wise investment, as it helps shield your car's interior from the sweltering heat.


  3. )  Check tyre pressure levels.

    Heat affects tyre pressure. High temperatures can pose a threat to your tyres, and during summers, low tyre pressure and hot roads can lead to tyre blowouts. So, to prevent any unwanted incidents, it is essential to ensure that your tyres are adequately inflated before driving in warm weather conditions. This simple measure will protect you from potential tyre blowouts and ensure your safe travels.


  4. )  Coating your car with a paint protective film.

    Investing in paint protection film for your vehicle is a wise decision as it provides an extra layer of defense against the damaging effects of the sun. This protective film not only safeguards your car from the sun's heat, but also shields it from scratches, dirt, and debris, thus ensuring its longevity and keeping it looking new.


  5. )  Regularly checking and servicing your battery.

    Battery problems are not limited to winter as summer can cause overheating and overcharging, leading to reduced battery life. It is important to remain vigilant and have your battery checked regularly during the summer season. Pay attention to any signs of battery issues and take action promptly.


  6. )  Checking your coolant level.

    It is crucial to prioritize checking your coolant levels, particularly during the summer season. Neglecting to do so may result in your vehicle overheating, which can cause your engine to sustain melted metal damage. Therefore, it is recommended to either have your fluids inspected by a professional or to perform the check yourself to prevent any potential issues before the scorching temperature sets in.


  7. )  Clothing your seats.

    Investing in seat covers is a wise decision as they serve the purpose of safeguarding your seats against stains, grime, and sun damage, thereby helping to maintain their pristine condition.


Importance of protecting your car paint.

A fresh car paint job is undoubtedly pleasing to the eyes. Although appearance is not everything, when it comes to cars, it holds a certain level of importance. A car is a significant investment, and naturally, you'd want it to look exceptional for a long time. This is why having a good quality car paint job is crucial. But to maintain that glossy paint finish, protecting it against damages is equally important, and paint protection can help with just that. So, it is essential to understand the significance of car paint jobs and how protection can help maintain the beauty of your car.

It is vital to safeguard your car's exterior to ensure that it remains visually appealing. Whether you are concerned about preventing scratches or preserving the vehicle's resale value, there are numerous benefits to maintaining your car's paint.

These benefits are discussed below;

  1. )  Reduces washing.

    Paint protection helps prevent your car's paint from fading, which can save you money in the long run. Dirt, dust and other particles can stick to your car's paint, and trying to clean it can result in damage to the paint job. With paint protection film, you can easily wipe away debris using thick cleaning cloth, instead of having to wash and scrub your car to restore its shine.

  2. )  Protection from scratches.

    Having a paint protection film on your car has numerous benefits, one of which is scratch protection. As cars age, they tend to accumulate scratches, dings, and other unsightly marks that, if left untreated, can lead to bigger problems down the line. Rust is also a major concern, especially in areas that receive heavy snowfall. Therefore, a paint protection film is an excellent way to safeguard your vehicle against cosmetic flaws and harmful rust.



  3. )  No need of waxing.

    Although wax can give your car a glossy and fresh appearance, it does not offer any long-lasting protection for the paint. Essentially, the protective paint layer is designed to provide a shiny finish from the outset, rendering the application of wax unnecessary.

  4. )  Protection from harsh weather conditions.

    Rust is a result of corrosion, which occurs when vehicles are exposed to harsh elements such as salt, rain, and snow. However, excessively hot weather conditions can also cause damage to a car's paint, leading to peeling and fading. To prevent such damage and ensure your car's exterior remains intact, it's advisable to consider using a car paint protection film. This offers reliable protection against the harmful effects of extreme weather, while also preserving the shine and appearance of your car's paint job.
